Framework & Marginal Analysis

  • Social Welfare Maximization: Optimal policy occurs where MB = MC.
  • MB Declines: Marginal Benefit of prison declines as population grows (society incarcerates the most dangerous offenders first).
  • If MC > MB → Over-incarceration (Reduce).
    If MB > MC → Under-incarceration (Increase).
  • Policy Question is Marginal: Not "Is prison good?" but "Does incarcerating the next person help?"
  • Sources of Policy Disagreement: Values (Normative), Data (Measurement differences), Causal Assumptions (Direction of policy effects).

Crime Measurement

  • UCR (Uniform Crime Reporting): Police data. Uses Hierarchy Rule (counts only most serious offense per incident), severely understating crime volume.
  • NIBRS: Replaced UCR. Detailed demographics, counts all offenses per incident. Created massive data gaps during the 2021 transition.
  • NCVS (Victimization Survey): Captures the Dark Figure of unreported crime. Excludes homicides, homeless, and children <12.
  • Homicide Primacy: Most studied because it is hard to hide, has a consistent definition, carries a huge VSL, and allows cross-country comparisons.
  • Clearance Rates: Homicide ~61.6% | Burglary & Auto ~13.5%.

Modeling Criminal Behavior

Becker Static Model (1968): One-time gamble.

E[Ucrime] = p × U(Y - f) + (1 - p) × U(Y)
  • p: Probability of getting caught.
  • Y: Payoff (monetary/psychic).
  • f: Penalty (fine, prison, stigma).
  • U(): Utility function (captures risk aversion).

Deterrence Levers:

  • ↑ p (Certainty): More police, surveillance, DNA databases.
  • ↑ L (Length): Higher time cost (Mandatory minimums).
  • ↑ f (Severity): Fines, civil disabilities, losing housing.
  • ↓ Y (Payoff): Disrupt stolen goods markets.

Lee & McCrary (2017): Age 18 threshold — adult charge probability jumps 20%→65%, but arrest rate unchanged. Implication: Youth have high discount rates (myopic). Raise p, not f.

The Social Burden of Crime

  • Tangible Costs: Medical bills, lost wages, property damage.
  • Intangible Costs: Trauma, pain, fear. Often dwarf tangible costs in violent crimes.
  • Valuation Methods:
    • Bottom-up: Summing tangible + civil jury awards.
    • Contingent Valuation: Surveys of WTP. Subject to hypothetical bias.
    • Hedonic Pricing: Real market transactions (wage premiums, housing prices).
Key Cost Statistics:
• VSL: ~$6.4M–$8M (revealed preferences, labor markets).
• Gross Cost of Crime: $3.2 Trillion/year.
• Net of Transfers: $1.65 Trillion/year.
• Public Spending: ~$350B  |  Private: ~$300B.

Causal Inference & Methods

ATE = E[ Yi(1) - Yi(0) ]
  • Fundamental Problem: Cannot observe both Y(1) and counterfactual Y(0) for the same unit.
  • Selection Bias: Positive (motivated participants → overestimates). Negative (struggling participants → underestimates).
  • SUTVA: Requires homogeneous treatment and no spillovers between units.
  • OVB: E[β̂] = βtrue + (effect of omitted on Y) × (corr. of omitted with D).
RCTRandom assignment eliminates selection bias. Gold standard.
DiDRequires Parallel Trends: treated & control follow same trajectory pre-policy.
IVZ must be relevant (F>10) and satisfy exclusion restriction. Yields LATE for compliers.
RDCompare units just above/below threshold. High internal, low external validity.

Criminal Legal System

  • Discretion at every stage: Police (stops/arrests) → Prosecutors (charges) → Judges (sentences). Small bias compounds across stages.
  • Bail Optimization:
minb E[ Ccrime·P(offense|b) + Cflight·P(flight|b) + Cdetain·P(detained|b) ]
  • Plea Bargaining: >90% of cases. Risk aversion + massive Trial Penalty drives guilty pleas even when innocent.
  • Aizer & Doyle (IV: judge harshness): Juvenile incarceration → +20pp adult prison (+62%), ↓ graduation. Criminogenic for marginal youth.
  • P(prison | crime): P(rep.)×P(arr.|rep.)×P(conv.|arr.)×P(prison|conv.) ≈ 0.075. Only 1 in 13 violent crimes leads to prison.

Policing: Staffing & Benefits

  • The Double Dividend (Chalfin 2025): 1% ↑ in police → significant ↓ in serious crime. Results in fewer serious arrests (because crimes are prevented).
  • Net Widening: While serious arrests fall, low-level "quality of life" arrests (liquor, traffic) rise drastically.
  • Hot Spots & Deterrence (Draca et al. 2011): London 7/7 bombings. Police surged then drawn down. Drawdown isolates police presence from psychological distress. Found 13% drop in susceptible crime.
  • Private Security Spillovers: Positive externalities (LoJack breaks up chop shops). Negative externalities (Visible alarms displace burglars to neighbors).

Policing: Costs & Disparities

  • Educational Costs (Ang 2020): Within 0.5mi of a police killing: GPA −0.08 SD, graduation −3.5pp.
  • Civilian Cooperation (Ang 2025): After George Floyd, 911 calls dropped relative to ShotSpotter → trust eroded, reporting collapsed.
  • Ferguson Effect (Devi & Fryer): DOJ investigations → abrupt de-policing → homicide spikes.
  • Commander Stops (Bacher-Hicks): Aggressive commanders ↑ stops and minor arrests, but zero effect on felony crime.
Racial Disparities & Benchmarking:
The Problem: Conditioning on "people stopped" is circular — the stop decision is already racially biased.
Hoekstra & Sloan (2020): 911 dispatch rotation → random officer race. White officers use force 50–60% more on identical calls, concentrated in minority neighborhoods.
