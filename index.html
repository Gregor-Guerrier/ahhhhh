Framework & Marginal Analysis

  • Social Welfare Maximization: Optimal policy where MB = MC.
  • MB Declines: As prison population grows, society incarcerates most dangerous first → MB falls.
  • If MC > MB → Over-incarceration (Reduce).   If MB > MC → Under-incarceration (Increase).
  • Policy Question is Marginal: Not "Is prison good?" but "Does incarcerating the next person help?"
  • Sources of Policy Disagreement: Values (normative), Data (measurement differences), Causal Assumptions (direction of effects).

Crime Measurement

  • UCR: Police data. Hierarchy Rule — counts only the most serious offense per incident → understates crime volume.
  • NIBRS: Replaced UCR 2021. Records all offenses per incident; richer data but created a transition gap.
  • NCVS: Victimization survey. Captures the Dark Figure of unreported crime. Excludes homicides, homeless, children <12.
  • Homicide Primacy: Hard to hide, consistent definition, huge VSL, cross-country comparable.
  • Clearance Rates: Homicide ~61.6%  |  Burglary & Auto ~13.5%.

Modeling Criminal Behavior (Becker 1968)

E[Ucrime] = p × U(Y − f) + (1 − p) × U(Y)  |  Commit iff > U(W)
  • p = probability of being caught  |  Y = payoff  |  f = penalty  |  W = legal wage

Deterrence Levers:

  • ↑ p (Certainty): More police, surveillance, DNA. Most effective for high-discount-rate offenders.
  • ↑ f (Severity): Fines, longer sentences, civil consequences.
  • ↓ Y (Payoff): Disrupt stolen goods markets.
  • ↑ W (Legal wage): Employment programs raise opportunity cost.

Lee & McCrary (2017): Age-18 threshold — adult charge prob. jumps 20%→65%, but arrest rate unchanged. Youth have high discount rates: raise p, not f.

Social Burden of Crime

  • Tangible: Medical, lost wages, property.  Intangible: Trauma, fear, PTSD — often dwarf tangibles.
  • Bottom-up: Tangibles + jury awards.  Contingent Valuation: Survey WTP (hypothetical bias).  Hedonic: Wage premiums, housing prices.
Key Numbers:
· VSL: ~$6.4M–$8M (revealed preferences, labor markets — not surveys).
· Gross cost of crime: ~$3.2T/yr  |  Net (minus transfers): ~$1.65T/yr.
· Public spending: ~$350B  |  Private security: ~$300B.
· Prison cost: ~$35k–$60k/inmate/yr.

Transfers: In theft, victim's loss = offender's gain → not a net social loss. Subtract from gross to get net cost. Homicide: no transfer, gross = net.

Causal Inference & Methods

ATE = E[Yi(1)−Yi(0)]  |  ATT = E[Yi(1)−Yi(0) | Di=1]  |  LATE = Cov(Y,Z)/Cov(D,Z)
  • Fundamental Problem: Can't observe Y(1) and Y(0) for same unit simultaneously.
  • OVB: E[β̂] = βtrue + (effect of omitted on Y) × (corr. of omitted with D).
  • SUTVA: No spillovers between units + homogeneous treatment.
  • IV conditions: (1) Relevance — Z predicts D (F>10). (2) Exclusion restriction — Z affects Y only through D (untestable, argued theoretically). LATE = effect for compliers only.
  • Parallel Trends (DID): Absent treatment, treated & control would have followed the same trajectory. Test with flat pre-period event study coefficients.
RCTRandom assignment → ATE. Gold standard. Watch for non-compliance/attrition.
DiDParallel trends req'd. Event study tests pre-trends. TWFE biased with staggered timing.
IVRelevance + exclusion restriction. LATE ≠ ATE — specify compliers.
RDJust above/below threshold. High internal, very low external validity. McCrary density test for manipulation.

Criminal Legal System

minb E[ Ccrime·P(offense|b) + Cflight·P(flight|b) + Cdetain·P(detained|b) ]
  • Discretion compounds: Police (stops) → Prosecutor (charges) → Judge (sentence). Small bias at each stage → large final disparity.
  • Plea Bargaining: >90% of cases. Risk aversion + massive Trial Penalty → defendants plead even if innocent.
  • Mandatory minimums: Sharp sentence discontinuities → used as natural experiments (RD design).
  • Collateral consequences: Lost employment, housing, voting rights → criminogenic post-release → ↓ legal wage W → ↑ recidivism in Becker model.
  • Aizer & Doyle (Judge IV): Juvenile incarceration → +20pp adult prison (+62%), ↓ graduation. Criminogenic for marginal youth.
  • P(prison|crime): P(rep.)×P(arr.)×P(conv.)×P(prison|conv.) ≈ 0.075. Only 1 in 13 violent crimes → prison.

Policing: Staffing & Benefits

  • Double Dividend (Chalfin 2025): 1% ↑ officers → ↓ serious crime AND ↓ serious arrests (prevention, not just enforcement). But ↑ low-level arrests = net widening.
  • Draca et al. (2011) — London 7/7: Police surge then 6-week drawdown. Distress & economic disruption persisted; only police reversed → clean isolation of deterrence effect. Found 13% drop in susceptible crime.
  • Hot Spots: ~1–3% of blocks generate ~50% of crime. Diffusion of benefits → crime often doesn't fully displace to nearby areas.
  • LoJack (Ayres & Levitt 1998): Hidden GPS = positive externality. All cars benefit; non-buyers free-ride → underprovided by market → justifies subsidy.
  • Visible alarms: Negative externality — displace burglars to neighbors. No net social benefit.

Policing: Costs & Disparities

  • Ang (2020): Within 0.5mi of police killing → GPA −0.08 SD (sustained), graduation −3.5pp. Mechanism: stress, distraction, institutional alienation.
  • Geller et al. (2014): Stops → PTSD in stopped individuals AND family members.
  • Ang et al. (2025): Post-George Floyd, 911/ShotSpotter ratio fell → trust eroded, reporting collapsed.
  • Ferguson Effect (Devi & Fryer): DOJ investigation → de-policing → homicide spike. Event study confirms causal chain.
  • Bacher-Hicks: Aggressive commanders ↑ stops → zero felony effect. Large community cost, near-zero serious crime benefit.
Racial Disparities & Benchmarking:
Problem: Conditioning on "people stopped" is circular — that decision is already biased.
Hoekstra & Sloan (2020): 911 dispatch rotation → officer race random (beat×time FE). White officers use 50–60% more force on identical calls, concentrated in minority neighborhoods.

Acronym & Key Term Glossary

ATE — Average Treatment Effect. E[Yi(1)−Yi(0)] across all units.
ATT — ATE on the Treated. Effect for treated units only (Di=1). Use for voluntary programs.
LATE — Local Average Treatment Effect. IV estimate; effect for compliers only. LATE ≠ ATE unless all are compliers.
OVB — Omitted Variable Bias. Excluded variable correlated with both treatment and outcome.
SUTVA — Stable Unit Treatment Value Assumption. (1) No spillovers; (2) Homogeneous treatment across all recipients.
DiD / DID — Difference-in-Differences. (YT,post−YT,pre) − (YC,post−YC,pre). Requires parallel trends.
RD — Regression Discontinuity. Compares units just above/below a threshold. High internal, low external validity.
RCT — Randomized Controlled Trial. Random assignment → balances observables & unobservables. Gold standard.
IV — Instrumental Variables. Z shifts D but not Y directly. Needs relevance (F>10) + exclusion restriction.
TWFE — Two-Way Fixed Effects. Unit FE + time FE. Biased with staggered treatment timing + heterogeneous effects.
UCR — Uniform Crime Reporting. Legacy FBI program (1930–2021). Hierarchy rule. Police-reported only.
NIBRS — National Incident-Based Reporting System. Replaced UCR 2021. All offenses per incident.
NCVS — National Crime Victimization Survey. Household survey. Captures unreported crime. No homicide/homeless/under-12.
VSL — Value of Statistical Life. ~$6.4M–$8M (2010$). From revealed preferences (labor market wage premiums). NOT surveys.
WTP — Willingness to Pay. Max $ a person would pay to avoid a harm. Basis for contingent valuation. Subject to hypothetical bias.
DWL — Deadweight Loss. Social waste from crime: resources spent on offense/defense produce no net value.
MB / MC — Marginal Benefit / Marginal Cost. Optimal policy: MB = MC. U.S. incarceration likely above optimum (MC > MB at margin).
ROR — Release on Recognizance. Pre-trial release with no financial conditions; signed promise to appear.
DOJ — Department of Justice. Conducts pattern-or-practice investigations of police departments.
GE — General Equilibrium. Economy-wide spillover effects. Classic SUTVA violation (e.g., LoJack, job training displacement).
OLS — Ordinary Least Squares. Standard regression. Unbiased only if no unobserved confounders correlated with treatment.
BW — Broken Windows. Policing theory: targeting minor disorder prevents serious crime. Challenged by Bacher-Hicks (zero felony effect).
PTSD — Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder. Documented community mental health cost of aggressive policing (Geller et al. 2014).
GPA — Grade Point Average. Falls −0.08 SD within 0.5mi of police killing (Ang 2020). Proxy for education cost of policing.
EU — Expected Utility. Probability-weighted average utility. Core of Becker rational-actor crime model.
FE — Fixed Effects. Controls for time-invariant unit characteristics. Beat×time FE key to Hoekstra & Sloan identification.
