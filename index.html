Framework & Marginal Analysis
  • MB = MC: Optimal policy. Not "Is prison good?" but "Does the next unit help?"
  • MB Declines: Society incarcerates most dangerous first → diminishing returns.
  • MC > MB → over-incarceration  |  MB > MC → under-incarceration.
  • Policy disagreement: Values (normative), Data (measurement), Causal direction.
  • Optimal punishment: p × f = social harm of crime.
Key numbers: ~2M incarcerated · ~4.5M on probation/parole · P(prison|crime committed) ≈ 0.075 · Prison cost: $35k–$60k/yr · 1 in 3 Black men born 2001 expected to be imprisoned.
Crime Measurement
  • UCR: Police-reported. Hierarchy Rule = most serious offense per incident only → undercounts. Replaced by NIBRS (2021).
  • NCVS: Victim survey. Captures Dark Figure of unreported crime. Excludes homicides, homeless, under-12.
  • NIBRS: Records all offenses per incident; caused 2021 data gap during transition.
  • Homicide primacy: Hard to hide, consistent definition, huge VSL, cross-country comparable. Dark figure ≈ zero.
  • Clearance rates: Homicide ~61.6% · Burglary/auto ~13.5%.
  • Stopping bias + recording bias: Both cause UCR to understate true crime levels.
Becker Model (1968) & Deterrence
E[U_crime] = p·U(Y−f) + (1−p)·U(Y) | Commit iff E[U] > U(W)
  • p = P(caught) · Y = payoff · f = penalty · W = legal wage
  • ↑ p (certainty): Most effective — hits every term. More police, DNA, surveillance.
  • ↑ f (severity): Only effective if offender is not highly present-biased.
  • ↑ W: Employment raises legal opportunity cost → ↓ crime margin.
  • ↓ Y: Disrupt stolen goods markets.
  • Risk averse: More deterred by f.  Risk loving: More deterred by p.

Lee & McCrary (2017): Age-18 → adult charge prob. jumps 20%→65%, zero arrest rate change. Youth have high discount rates → raise p, not f.

Social Burden of Crime
  • Tangible: Medical, lost wages, property damage.
  • Intangible: Trauma, fear, PTSD. Dominate for violent crime.
  • Hedonic / revealed preference: Labor market wage premiums → VSL. Best method (not surveys).
  • Contingent valuation: WTP surveys. Subject to hypothetical bias.
  • Transfers: Theft = victim loss + offender gain → net social cost is intangibles only. Homicide = no transfer; gross = net.
VSL ~$6.4M–$8M (labor market) · Gross cost ~$3.2T/yr · Net ~$1.65T/yr · Public spending ~$350B · Private security ~$300B.
Causal Inference & Methods
ATE=E[Yi(1)−Yi(0)] ATT=E[Yi(1)−Yi(0)|Di=1] LATE=Cov(Y,Z)/Cov(D,Z)
OVB: E[β̂] = β_true + (effect of omitted on Y)×(corr. of omitted with D)
  • SUTVA: No spillovers + homogeneous treatment.
  • IV conditions: (1) Relevance — Z→D (F>10, testable). (2) Exclusion restriction — Z affects Y only via D (untestable). LATE = compliers only.
  • Parallel trends: Absent treatment, treated & control follow same trajectory. Test: pre-period βk ≈ 0.
  • Compliers: Take treatment iff Z=1. Always-takers and never-takers excluded from LATE.

Criminal Legal System
min_b E[C_crime·P(offense|b) + C_flight·P(flight|b) + C_detain·P(detained|b)]
  • Discretion compounds: Police (stops) → Prosecutor (charges) → Judge (sentences). Small bias at each stage → large final disparity.
  • Plea bargaining: >90% of cases. Risk aversion + Trial Penalty → pleas even when innocent.
  • Collateral consequences: Employment, housing, voting rights lost → ↑ recidivism via ↓ W.
  • Aizer & Doyle (judge IV): Juvenile incarceration → +20pp adult prison (+62%), ↓ graduation. Criminogenic for marginal youth.
  • RD on mandatory mins: Test manipulation with McCrary density test (histogram of running var).
Policing: Staffing & Benefits
  • Chalfin (2025): 1% ↑ officers → ↓ serious crime + ↓ serious arrests (prevention). But ↑ low-level arrests = net widening.
  • Draca et al. (2011) — London 7/7: Surge then drawdown. Distress persisted; only police reversed → clean deterrence. 13% drop in susceptible crime.
  • Hot spots: ~1–3% of blocks = ~50% of crime. Diffusion of benefits → crime doesn't fully displace.
  • LoJack (Ayres & Levitt 1998): Hidden GPS = positive externality. All cars benefit; non-buyers free-ride → underprovided → subsidy justified.
  • Visible alarms: Negative externality — displace burglars to neighbors. No net social benefit.
Policing: Costs & Disparities
  • Ang (2020): Within 0.5mi of police killing → GPA −0.08 SD, grad −3.5pp. Mechanism: stress, alienation.
  • Geller et al. (2014): Stops → PTSD in individuals + family members.
  • Ang et al. (2025): Post-George Floyd, 911/ShotSpotter ratio ↓ → trust eroded, reporting collapsed.
  • Ferguson Effect (Devi & Fryer): DOJ investigations → de-policing → homicide spike. Event study confirms causal chain.
  • Bacher-Hicks: Aggressive commanders ↑ stops → zero felony effect. Large community cost, zero serious crime benefit.
Benchmarking problem: Conditioning on "people stopped" is circular — stops are already biased.
Hoekstra & Sloan (2020): 911 dispatch rotation → officer race random (beat×time FE). White officers use 50–60% more force on identical calls, in minority neighborhoods.
Acronym & Key Term Glossary
ATE — Avg Treatment Effect: E[Yi(1)−Yi(0)]. All units.
ATT — ATE on the Treated: effect for Di=1 only. Use for voluntary programs.
LATE — Local ATE: IV estimate for compliers only. LATE ≠ ATE unless all comply.
OVB — Omitted Variable Bias: excluded var correlated with both D and Y.
SUTVA — Stable Unit Treatment Value Assumption: (1) no spillovers; (2) homogeneous treatment.
DiD / DID — Difference-in-Differences: (Y_T,post−Y_T,pre)−(Y_C,post−Y_C,pre). Needs parallel trends.
RD — Regression Discontinuity: compare units just above/below threshold. Low external validity.
RCT — Randomized Controlled Trial: random assignment → ATE. Gold standard.
IV — Instrumental Variable: Z→D but Z↛Y directly. Needs relevance (F>10) + exclusion restriction.
TWFE — Two-Way Fixed Effects: unit FE + time FE. Biased under staggered timing + heterogeneous effects.
FE — Fixed Effects: controls for time-invariant unit traits. Beat×time FE key to Hoekstra & Sloan.
OLS — Ordinary Least Squares: unbiased only if no unobserved confounders correlated with treatment.
UCR — Uniform Crime Reporting: legacy FBI (1930–2021). Hierarchy rule. Police-reported only.
NIBRS — National Incident-Based Reporting: replaced UCR 2021. All offenses per incident.
NCVS — National Crime Victimization Survey: household survey capturing unreported crime.
VSL — Value of Statistical Life: ~$6.4M–$8M from labor market wage premiums. NOT surveys.
WTP — Willingness to Pay: max $ to avoid harm. Contingent valuation. Subject to hypothetical bias.
DWL — Deadweight Loss: social waste from offense/defense arms race; no net value produced.
MB / MC — Marginal Benefit / Cost: optimal policy at MB=MC. U.S. incarceration likely MC>MB at margin.
GE — General Equilibrium: economy-wide spillovers; classic SUTVA violation (LoJack, job training).
DOJ — Dept. of Justice: pattern-or-practice investigations → Ferguson Effect (Devi & Fryer).
ROR — Release on Recognizance: pre-trial release, no financial conditions, signed promise to appear.
BW — Broken Windows: targeting minor disorder prevents serious crime. Challenged by Bacher-Hicks (zero felony effect).
PTSD — Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder: documented cost of aggressive policing (Geller et al. 2014).
EU — Expected Utility: probability-weighted utility. Core of Becker rational-actor model.
GPA — Grade Point Average: proxy for education cost of policing. Falls −0.08 SD within 0.5mi of killing (Ang 2020).
McCrary test — Density test of running variable at RD threshold. Discontinuity → manipulation → RD invalid.
